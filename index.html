Biography

Bradley Mikesell II (b. 1998) is a composer and trumpeter currently based in State College, Pennsylvania. Recent musical interests of Bradley include the idea of authenticity in composition, performance, and social settings.

Their music has been performed by numerous groups such as The Florida Orchestra, loadbang, Transient Canvas, Quasar Saxophone Quartet, Corvus, and other chamber groups. They have also been the recipient of several honors and awards, including having been a finalist in the 2021 Morton Gould Young Composer Awards and a winner of multiple call for scores. Some of these include the 2021 Penn State Chamber Winds Call for Scores, a reading session and worship with The Florida Orchestra in 2017 and 2020, the 2018 Terroir New Music Call for Scores, and others.

Bradley has the M.M. degree in Music Composition from Pennsylvania State University and the B.M. degree in Acoustic and Electronic Music Composition at the University of South Florida and is currently pursuing the PPC certificate in Composition at PSU.
