ABOUT ME

Brianna Bee Mikesell (b. 1998) is a composer whose diverse body of work encompasses compositions for solo instruments, ensembles of varying sizes, and electronic music. Her works often intricately incorporate physical space, improvisational elements, and data sonification. A central focus of hers lies in leveraging live audio processing to enhance live performances. An embodiment of this interest is her ongoing project, "Emissary," an innovative web-based platform designed to facilitate interactive real-time collaborations between musicians and electronics.

Her music has been performed by numerous groups such as The Florida Orchestra, loadbang, Transient Canvas, Quasar Saxophone Quartet, Corvus, and other notable groups. She was a finalist in the 2021 Morton Gould Young Composer Awards and has won various other call-for-score competitions such as the 2021 Penn State Chamber Winds Call for Scores, performances with The Florida Orchestra in both 2017 and 2020, the 2018 Terroir New Music Call for Scores, among others.

In 2022, she received a Master of Music degree in Music Composition from Penn State University from under Dr. Baljinder Sekhon and Dr. Sarah Genevieve Burghart Rice. She also holds a Bachelor of Music degree in Acoustic and Electronic Music Composition at the University of South Florida.
